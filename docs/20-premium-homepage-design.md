# 20 - Premium Homepage Design

## 1. Homepage Experience Goal

The homepage should immediately communicate premium fitness, credible coaching, clear membership paths, and operational polish. It must convert visitors without feeling like a template landing page.

Primary CTA: Book Free Trial  
Secondary CTA: View Memberships

## 2. Homepage Section Order

1. Hero
2. Brand Introduction
3. Fitness Programs
4. Membership Plans
5. Trainer Showcase
6. Transformation Stories
7. Testimonials
8. Facilities
9. Class Schedule Preview
10. Gallery
11. FAQ
12. Contact / Final CTA

## 3. Hero Section

### Layout

- Full first viewport but leave a hint of the next section visible on desktop and mobile.
- Use real high-quality gym/facility/training image or short muted video as background.
- Place headline and CTA over media, not inside a card.
- Add subtle overlay from obsidian to transparent for readability.
- Right or lower area can show a compact live-style proof strip: members, classes, rating, open hours.

### Content

Headline:

"Train with intent. Move with power."

Subheading:

"A premium fitness club built for strength, conditioning, mobility, and consistent progress, with expert coaches and a member experience that keeps every step clear."

Primary CTA:

"Book Free Trial"

Secondary CTA:

"View Memberships"

Proof points:

- Coach-led programs
- Flexible memberships
- Modern strength and conditioning zones
- Digital member portal

### Components

- Public header.
- Hero headline.
- CTA button group.
- Proof strip.
- Scroll cue.
- Background media.

### Animation Strategy

- Header fades in immediately.
- Hero headline fades and moves up 12px over 500ms.
- CTA group appears 80ms after headline.
- Proof strip appears 160ms after CTA.
- Background image should not scale continuously; avoid heavy parallax.

## 4. Brand Introduction

### Layout

- Two-column desktop layout.
- Left: concise brand statement.
- Right: three metric/proof tiles.
- Mobile: stacked text followed by horizontal stat cards or two-column compact grid.

### Content

Eyebrow:

"Built for measurable training"

Heading:

"A club experience designed around consistency, coaching, and clarity."

Body:

"Apex Performance Club brings together modern equipment, structured programs, experienced trainers, and a digital member portal so every visit feels purposeful from check-in to renewal."

Proof tiles:

- "6 training zones" - Strength, conditioning, mobility, recovery, functional, personal coaching.
- "Coach-led plans" - Programs adapted to your goals and level.
- "Simple membership" - Transparent plans, online payments, and renewal reminders.

CTA:

"Explore Programs"

### Animation Strategy

- Section content reveals on scroll with small stagger.
- Stat tiles count up only if implemented accessibly and without layout shift.

- Section intro.
- Four to six program cards with real images or strong cropped action photos.
- Cards use compact labels, program outcome, and CTA.

### Program Cards

| Program | Description | CTA |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Strength Training | Build muscle, confidence, and technique with progressive coaching. | View Strength |
| HIIT Conditioning | High-output sessions for stamina, fat loss, and athletic energy. | View HIIT |
| Personal Training | One-to-one coaching with structure, accountability, and measurable progress. | Meet Coaches |
| Mobility and Recovery | Move better, reduce stiffness, and support long-term performance. | Learn More |
| Weight Management | Practical training and nutrition guidance for sustainable change. | Start Today |
| Group Classes | Instructor-led classes that keep training social, focused, and consistent. | See Schedule |

### Animation Strategy

- Cards fade up on scroll in groups.
- Hover reveals CTA and adds subtle image zoom within reserved frame.
- No layout shift on hover.

- Highlight recommended plan with premium badge.
- Include "best for" text, price, duration, benefits, and CTA.

### Content

Section heading:

"Memberships that match how you train."

Section body:

"Choose a plan built for your rhythm, from flexible monthly access to committed transformation programs with coaching support."

Plan examples:

| Plan | Best For | Description | CTA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Essential Monthly | Getting started | Flexible access to the gym floor, core facilities, and member portal. | Choose Essential |
| Performance Quarterly | Building routine | A structured 90-day commitment with better value and renewal reminders. | Choose Performance |
| Elite Annual | Long-term results | The best value for members ready to train consistently all year. | Choose Elite |

CTA:

"Compare All Plans"

### Animation Strategy

- Recommended card can enter with subtle emphasis, not bounce.
- Price values should not animate in a way that delays readability.

## 8. Transformation Stories

### Layout

- Editorial split layout.
- Left: transformation story narrative.
- Right: before/after or milestone cards if real assets are available.
- If before/after images are unavailable, use measurable story cards instead of fake images.

### Content

Heading:

"Progress you can feel, track, and repeat."

Story:

"From first-week confidence to long-term strength, our members build routines that fit real schedules. Trainers help set the plan, attendance keeps progress visible, and the portal keeps membership details simple."

Metrics:

- "12-week consistency blocks"
- "Trainer-guided workout plans"
- "Attendance visibility for better habits"

CTA:

"Start Your Trial"

### Animation Strategy

- Metrics reveal one by one.
- Avoid dramatic before/after animation unless real consented assets exist.

## 10. Facilities

### Layout

- Full-width image-led section with facility zones.
- Use real images of equipment, training floor, lockers, recovery area.
- Desktop: large image grid with captions.
- Mobile: vertical image cards.

### Content

Heading:

"Premium facilities, built for focused training."

Facility bullets:

- Strength floor with modern racks, benches, and free weights.
- Conditioning zone for high-output training.
- Functional training space for mobility and athletic movement.
- Clean changing areas and member-friendly amenities.
- Coach-led spaces for personal training and group sessions.

CTA:

"View Gallery"

### Animation Strategy

- Lazy-load images.
- Caption appears with subtle opacity on hover/focus.

## 12. Gallery

### Layout

- Masonry-like but stable grid with defined aspect ratios.
- Use 6 to 8 images.
- Include categories: Strength Floor, Classes, Personal Training, Facilities.

### Content

Heading:

"See the space before you step in."

CTA:

"Open Gallery"

### Animation Strategy

- Lazy image reveal with blur-up or fade only.
- Avoid layout-changing masonry; use CSS grid with fixed aspect ratios.

## 13. FAQ

### Layout

- Accordion list.
- Include plan, trial, payment, trainer, class, and cancellation questions.

### Content

Heading:

"Everything you need to know before your first session."

CTA:

"Still have a question? Talk to us"

### Animation Strategy

- Accordion opens under 220ms.
- Respect reduced motion.

## 14. Contact / Final CTA

### Layout

- Strong final conversion band with contact details.
- Use map/directions link, WhatsApp, phone, hours, and compact form link.
- Avoid enclosing the entire section in a card.

### Content

Heading:

"Your strongest routine can start this week."

Body:

"Book a free trial, visit the club, or speak with our team about the right membership for your goals."

Primary CTA:

"Book Free Trial"

Secondary CTA:

"WhatsApp Us"

Details:

- Open daily
- Flexible plans
- Coach-led onboarding
- Online payments and renewals

### Animation Strategy

- Final CTA appears with simple fade.
- Buttons use interaction feedback only.

## 15. Homepage Performance Notes

- Use one priority image/video only in hero.
- Do not load Razorpay, charts, admin scripts, or portal-only JS on homepage.
- Lazy-load gallery and below-fold media.
- Use static/cached rendering and revalidate when CMS content changes.
- Avoid auto-playing heavy video on mobile unless compressed and optional.
- Reserve all image dimensions to prevent CLS.
